Workplace (R)evolution 2014 is about all the ways to get the most out of your working life. It’s about living healthier, working smarter and growing better together. And we won’t be afraid to tackle the uncomfortable topics – think cultural competency, balancing generational differences and why the traditional corporate hierarchy might just not cut it anymore.

This one-day event will inspire your team, challenge traditional practices and provide actionable tools that can be taken back to any organizational setting – from established Fortune 500 company to new small business or start-up. Workplace (R)evolution 2014 will open your mind, give you the tools you need to make your workplace better and might just make Monday morning your favorite time of the week.

Join the (R)evolution.

Speakers

Steve L. Robbins, Ph.D.

A thought leader and innovator, Dr. Robbins' unique concept of "Unintentional Intolerance" has captured wide acclaim from numerous audiences and organizations across the United States. His approach does NOT blame or point fingers. It uses neuroscience and the science of human behavior to challenge individuals and organizations to be more open-minded, mindful and intentional about inclusion and valuing people for their unique gifts, abilities and experiences.

Richard Sheridan

From kid programmer in 1971 to Forbes cover story in 2003, Joy, Inc. author Richard Sheridan (U-M grad BS Computer Science '80, MS Computer Engineering '82) has never shied from challenges, opportunities nor the limelight. While his focus has always been around technology, his passion is actually process, teamwork and organizational design, with one inordinately popular goal: the Business Value of Joy!

Chuck Blakeman

Author of the #1 Business Book of 2010 Making Money is Killing Your Business and his new Top Ten Business Book of 2013 Why Employees are ALWAYS a Bad Idea Mr. Blakeman is a results leader, and has decades of sales, marketing and operations experience leading companies in marketing, import/export, fulfillment, call centers, website development, printing and direct mail processing.

Gigi Durham

Meenakshi Gigi Durham's work centers on media and the politics of the body, with an emphasis on gender, sexuality, race, and youth cultures. She holds a joint appointment in the Department of Gender, Women's and Sexuality Studies. She was named a CLAS Collegiate Scholar in 2012.

Michael Kutcher

Michael Kutcher, twin brother of actor Ashton Kutcher, is an effective national cerebral palsy advocate and a passionate motivational speaker. Michael's inspirational message is uplifting and stimulating to all as he promotes hope and courage through his life’s story of overcoming challenges and defeating extraordinary odds.
